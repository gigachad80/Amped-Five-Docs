### 2.2 INTERLACE MODULE
**Purpose**: Handle interlaced video formats (analog, older DVRs)

#### **Filters & Sub-Filters**:

**Deinterlace**
- **What it does**: Converts interlaced video (alternating scan lines) to progressive
- **When to use**: Visible horizontal scan lines, combing artifacts, 480i/576i/1080i sources
- **Key parameters**: Method (Weave/Bob/Blend/Auto), field order (top/bottom first)
- **Situation**: Analog VCR tape evidence, broadcast television recording, older CCTV systems
- **Example**: VHS tape evidence - "Deinterlace → Method: Bob (for motion) → Field order: Bottom first → Eliminates combing artifacts"
- **Technical detail**: Interlaced video contains odd/even scan line fields captured at different times; deinterlacing merges or separates these fields
- **Workflow position**: Apply FIRST before any enhancement filters
- **Caution**: Do NOT use on progressive video (1080p, 4K) - will degrade quality

**Field Separator**
- **What it does**: Separates odd and even interlaced fields into individual frames
- **When to use**: Need field-level temporal analysis, slow-motion extraction
- **Key parameters**: Field order, output format
- **Situation**: Fast-moving object analysis requiring maximum temporal resolution
- **Example**: Traffic accident reconstruction - "Field Separator → Doubles effective frame rate → 30fps becomes 60 fields/second → Captures vehicle position with 16.7ms temporal precision instead of 33.3ms"
- **Result**: 2× temporal resolution increase for motion analysis
- **Specialized use**: Forensic timing analysis, high-speed event capture
